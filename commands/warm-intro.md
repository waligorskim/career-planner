You are drafting a warm introduction request. This is the highest-conversion outreach method available — referral hires convert at 5–10× vs. cold applications. The message has to be specific enough that the mutual connection doesn't have to think hard, and valuable enough that they actually want to send it.

The mutual connection is doing Mateusz a favour. Make it easy for them. Never make them feel obligated.

## Step 1: Parse the request

From `$ARGUMENTS`, extract:
- **Target person**: who Mateusz wants to reach (name, role, company)
- **Mutual connection**: who will make the introduction
- **Context**: why Mateusz wants to connect (is there a specific role? general interest in the company? shared domain?)

If the mutual connection or target is unclear, ask before drafting.

## Step 2: Research the target

Run 1–2 WebSearch queries:
- `[target person name] [company] LinkedIn`
- `[target person name] [company] background`

Find: their role, how long they've been there, any public work (talks, posts, articles) that creates a genuine connection point with Mateusz's background.

## Step 3: Load Mateusz's context

Read `career-facts.md` to identify:
- Shared domain with the target (analytics, AI, product, UX, gaming, etc.)
- Relevant achievement that's most credible to this specific person/company
- Any existing connection signals (e.g. if target is at a Google-partnered company, the Gemini case study is relevant)

## Step 4: Draft two messages

**Message A — to the mutual connection (requesting the intro)**

Structure (150 words max):
- Open with something human (not "I hope this finds you well")
- State the ask clearly in the first 2 sentences — don't bury it
- Why THIS person specifically — one genuine, specific reason
- What Mateusz is exploring — honest and non-desperate (he's passively open, not urgently looking)
- Make it easy to say yes AND easy to say no — remove friction both ways
- Specific ask: "Would you be comfortable making an email intro?" — not "would love if you could connect us somehow"

**Message B — the intro blurb (to be forwarded)**

Structure (100 words max):
- 2 sentences on who Mateusz is — the most relevant thing for this specific person
- 1 sentence on why this connection makes sense for both parties
- 1 sentence soft ask: "Would love 20 minutes if you're open to it"
- Mateusz's contact details

## Step 5: Output

Present both messages clearly labelled.

After both messages, include:
- **Why this framing**: 2–3 bullets on the strategic choices made
- **What to watch for**: any risks (e.g. if mutual connection has a complex relationship with the target, the intro may do more harm than good)

End with: *"Review before sending to [mutual connection name]. Adjust any personal details or tone to match your relationship with them — you know them better than I do."*
